Clients

Viderent works with clients who seek sustainable growth through rapid innovation. Our clients vary from the Fortune 500 to large private companies, to not-for-profit enterprises, and government agencies. Many of our clients describe themselves in one of the following ways:

We were founded on a core platform technology. We grew rapidly for years, but in the past 24 months have seen our growth slow down. We may be facing a plateau with our current technology solutions and customers.

Our knowledge of our customers’ and their needs have been our core advantage. But recently we’ve realized that we don’t know our newer customers so well.

Operational excellence for us is working with our channel partners and distributors. They’ve been so important to our success, but may be blocking our growth now!

Innovation is an important concept for us. But our internal efforts don’t seem to be producing the results we need. We had lots of good ideas, but nothing seemed to come of them.

Employees comment that they feel that good ideas don’t advance quickly. We think that we have the best employees in the world, but they are getting frustrated by the corporate structure and approvals process.

We used to be innovative. One person and a good idea could really move the ball. But today, everything takes so long – and it’s so political.

The bureaucracy here can be really stifling and we are struggling to create impactful change.

We need to motivate and stimulate our staff. Giving them the chance to test their ideas in a structured way has made it more fun and productive.

We have had an innovation program for years. This is the first time we’ve been able to show any benefits though!

We have lots of ideas around here. What you’ve given us a scalable process. This will completely change our ideas for innovation!

Services

Rapid Experimentation and Innovation (REXIN)


Create viable ideas by re-imagining and testing the skills and expertise of your staff and internal resources. Viderent leads groups of 5 to 50 individuals or teams in workshops which create new opportunities, lateral thinking and executable projects.

Discover and characterize the customers for your projects. Refine the value propositions that make them jump out of their chairs with excitement. Pivot quickly to new opportunities as you develop the business model for your project. Develop working prototypes to further refine your understanding of the customer and product. Discover which features change customer behavior, make them lean-in, and go beyond ‘liking it’ to loving it. Find the best ways to scale up rapidly, and drive into the market.

Discover and develop the key stakeholders to enable change, and design your stakeholder management plan. Identify key partners, customers and licensors. Develop train-the-trainer program to cascade the skills and processes throughout the organization.

Focus on process control in order to modify and pivot on the fly. We identify key metrics for success and develop processes for monitoring them. We survey, interview and measure behavioral change in your teams and projects.

Whitney Hischier

Partner

Whitney Hischier specializes in helping organizations and individuals simplify complex problems. She is a lecturer at the Haas School of Business where she works with MBAs, undergraduates and executives on innovation programs, coaching and consulting. Whitney is also an NSF i-Corp trained instructor. She has worked extensively with corporations, governments and startups to help them effectively scale-up their enterpreneurial activities.

Clients include the Polish, Hungarian, Kuwaiti and Panamanian Ministries of Science and Technology, Los Alamos National Lab, Berkeley National Lab, Livermore National Lab, Statoil, Mubadala Group, Dar-Al Hekma University, American University of Mongolia, Newcom Group Mongolia, NetApp, to name a few.

Prior to teaching, Whitney served as Assistant Dean of Executive Education at UC Berkeley for nearly a decade. During her tenure, she grew program revenues 8x and used her background in management consulting at Deloitte and KPMG to help clients build programs that addressed specific business and individual performance gaps.

Whitney has a BA in International Relations with a focus on Security and Arms Control from Stanford University and an MBA with a Certificate in Healthcare Management from the Haas School of Business, UC Berkeley. She lives in Berkeley, CA with her husband, two children, three Ridgeback dogs, two cats and horse.

Todd Morrill

Partner

T. Todd Morrill is a founder and Managing Partner at Viderent Partners. He is the Faculty Director at the Bay Area I-Corps Node and a National Instructor for the NSF I-Corps, the NIH I-Corps, and the I-Corps for Educators. He has taught the I-Corps LLP/BMC 8 week syllabus more than 30 times to cohorts in technology, services, bio/medicine, financial services, retail and manufacturing. Todd specializes in Corporate programs, where he designs and delivers 8 week innovation immersions to Fortune 500 companies.

Todd has launched major product lines himself, at large and small software and bio/medical companies. His has taught corporate teams the 8 week immersion program, followed by 28 week MVP and go-to-market training, ending with product launch.

He has been building and leading companies since the first biotech boom in the early 1980s. Todd's experience includes a variety of VC-backed and large companies, and he was a founder of three. He spent seven years in investment banking and has worked at multinational pharma, diagnostics and tools companies. His roles have included sales, product management, marketing, R&D, business development and CEO. He has been an independent Board member of two venture-backed companies.

Todd has taught at the Haas School of Business, University of California, Berkeley since 2001, where he is a Richard H. Holton Teaching Fellow and a Senior Fellow at the Lester Center for Entrepreneurship. He teaches at Berkeley, UCSF, and UTexas, and teaches innovation to executives worldwide through Berkeley and Viderent Partners. He has taught in the Intel Program in China and Japan, the Malaysia/UCSF program, and in programs in Estonia, Australia and Kuwait. Todd received an MBA from Haas and his BA in Biology, with Highest Honors, from Dartmouth College.
